# Article Spec

- **Topic**: Why I built TAT (Tiny AI Team) — structured multi-model AI workflow instead of autonomous coding
- **Platform**: LinkedIn article
- **Audience**: Engineers, founders, tech leads who use AI coding tools
- **Key message**: The problem with autonomous AI coding isn't intelligence — it's the lack of process, roles, and memory
- **Tone**: Personal, honest, conversational — not salesy
- **Length**: ~1200 words (LinkedIn article)

## Outline
1. Hook: autonomous AI coding sounds great but frustrated me
2. The real problems: no structure, no memory, no roles
3. Software is built by teams, not solo brains
4. What I built: TAT — roles, workflow, memory
5. The real insight: every lesson becomes part of the process
6. Final thought: the future is AI teams with human leads

## Image Direction
- Core visual metaphor: small team of distinct AI agents working together at a table, with a human at the head
- Style: flat, minimal, professional illustration
- Palette: muted blues and grays with one warm accent color
